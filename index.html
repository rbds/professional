Research

My research is in energy-focused navigation algorithms for mobile robots. Much of the work takes place in MATLAB simulations, for which you can find the code here: https://github.com/rbds/Research. We also have a robotic test platform which uses an Arduino for control and a Raspberry Pi for computing to test out our algorithms in real environments.

In the spirit of Thing Explainer (https://xkcd.com/1133/ uses the one thousand most common words in English to explain complex mechanisms, machines, and ideas): I help tell moving machines how to get from place to place without running into anything and without running out of power.

Numerical Methods

This work was completed as a part of the Numerical Methods class at GWU. For my final project, I investigated different how numerical integration techniques can be applied to analyzing the motion of physical systems. Using single and double pendulums, I looked at how deriving the equations of motion from the Hamiltonian and the Lagrangian, and the respective methods of integrations, can affect the behavior of a simulated system. If you are interested in numerical integration, symplectic integrators, or mechanics, please take a look!
